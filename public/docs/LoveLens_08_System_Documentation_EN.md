# 💕 What is LoveLens

LoveLens is a **relationship style test**.

It is **not**: a "who-matches-whom" compatibility test, not a personality test, not a tool to predict your future relationships.

It **is**: a mirror—helping you see **what you're like in a relationship**. How you express love, how close you want two people to be, where you go when you face conflict, and what your deepest feelings about intimacy are.

The core assumption of LoveLens is: **every person in a relationship loves in their own way**. There's no "right way" or "wrong way"—only **different ways**. Understanding your own way, and understanding your partner's way, is the starting point for two people to be better together.

### 🌿 Why It's Called LoveLens

**Lens** means a camera lens. LoveLens is "the lens of love"—through this lens, what you see isn't "what you should be like," but **"what you actually are like"**.

A good lens doesn't change the person being photographed—it just lets that person **be seen more clearly**. That's what LoveLens aims to do.

# 📐 The Four Dimensions Explained

LoveLens looks at a person in a relationship through **4 dimensions**. Each dimension measures an independent question, and the answers don't influence each other.

---

## 🔵 Dimension 1: Attachment

**What this dimension measures:** What's your deepest feeling about "intimate relationships"? When you get close to someone, what's the basic state of your inner world?

This is the deepest dimension in the entire test—it influences all your other behaviors.

### The 4 Letters

| Letter | Name | Core Feeling |
|--------|------|--------------|
| **S** | Secure | I can be close and independent, both feel okay |
| **A** | Anxious | I long to be loved, but I fear losing |
| **V** | aVoidant | I value independence, I'm wary of being consumed |
| **X** | disorganiXed | I want to get close and run away at the same time |

### Where These Letters Come From

Attachment Theory is one of the most important theories in modern psychology about intimate relationships, developed by John Bowlby and Mary Ainsworth in the 20th century. The attachment dimension of LoveLens is **based on this theory but described in a more everyday way**—you don't need to understand psychology to know which one you are.

### 🌙 The Most Important Thing About This Dimension

**Attachment type is not destiny.** A person can slowly move from anxious to secure, or gradually open up from avoidant. Test results show what you're like **right now**—not what you'll be forever.

---

## 🟢 Dimension 2: Expression

**What this dimension measures:** How do you let your love **be seen**? Where does your love mainly live?

Two people can love each other, but if love isn't expressed and the other person can't feel it—that relationship will slowly wither. This dimension looks at **how you transmit love outward**.

### The 4 Letters

| Letter | Name | Primary Language of Love |
|--------|------|--------------------------|
| **W** | Words | I love through words—"I love you," "I miss you," "Thank you" |
| **D** | Doing | I love through actions—quietly preparing, remembering details, practical giving |
| **P** | Presence | I love through being there—not necessarily talking, but I'm there |
| **F** | Frame | I love through structure—seriously managing the relationship, clearly discussing expectations |

### Where These Letters Come From

The expression dimension is inspired by Gary Chapman's "5 Love Languages" theory, but LoveLens **simplified and reorganized** it—merging the original 5 into 4, more focused and more suited to East Asian emotional expression.

### 🌙 The Most Important Thing About This Dimension

**No expression style is more "advanced" than another.** Words-people aren't more romantic than Doing-people, and Frame-people aren't colder than Presence-people. The question is simply: **whether your expression style matches your partner's receiving style**.

---

## 🟡 Dimension 3: Structure

**What this dimension measures:** What **shape** do you want the relationship between two people to be? What are the distance and connection between you two like?

Some people like two people becoming one, some like walking their own paths. This dimension looks at your ideal relationship structure.

### The 4 Letters

| Letter | Name | Relationship Shape |
|--------|------|---------------------|
| **M** | Merging | We become one, sharing everything |
| **N** | Nested | We have overlap, and each has our own space |
| **L** | paraLlel | We walk our own paths, side by side |
| **O** | Orbit | I'm in your life, but I keep my distance |

### Where These Letters Come From

The structure dimension is **LoveLens's own creation**—inspired by Esther Perel's relationship research and Terrence Real's "Connection Spectrum," but described using more intuitive **spatial metaphors**.

### 🌙 The Most Important Thing About This Dimension

**All four shapes have their own beauty.** Merging relationships have the depth of merging; parallel relationships have the respect of parallelism. The key isn't **which one is "better"**—it's **whether two people's shapes can match**—or whether they can adjust for each other.

---

## 🔴 Dimension 4: Conflict

**What this dimension measures:** When problems arise in the relationship and you have disagreements, **which direction do you go**?

Conflict in relationships is inevitable. What truly determines relationship quality isn't "whether there's conflict," but **how you handle conflict**.

### The 4 Letters

| Letter | Name | Default Response in Conflict |
|--------|------|------------------------------|
| **C** | Confront | If there's a problem, let's talk—the sooner the better |
| **T** | Think | I need time to think on my own |
| **R** | Repair | Let's warm the atmosphere first, then talk slowly |
| **Y** | Yield | Let it go, the relationship matters more than being right |

### Where These Letters Come From

The conflict dimension is inspired by John Gottman's marriage research. Gottman spent decades studying how couples fight and found that **what determines whether a relationship can last isn't "not fighting" but "how you fight"**. LoveLens simplified his research into 4 core patterns.

### 🌙 The Most Important Thing About This Dimension

**All 4 conflict patterns have healthy and unhealthy versions.** Confronting can be the maturity of "let's talk when there's a problem," or the attack of "I have to win." Yielding can be the wisdom of "the relationship matters more than being right," or the avoidance of "I fear conflict." **The difference isn't which one you use, but whether you're using its healthy or unhealthy side**.

# 🎨 The Code System

## 🔤 Basic Code

Your LoveLens code consists of **4 letters**, one from each dimension:

```
[Attachment] - [Expression] - [Structure] - [Conflict]
     S       -      F       -      N      -     C
```

### Examples

- **S-F-N-C** = Secure + Frame expression + Nested structure + Confront conflict
- **A-W-M-R** = Anxious + Words expression + Merging structure + Repair conflict
- **V-P-L-Y** = Avoidant + Presence expression + Parallel structure + Yield conflict

There are a total of **4 × 4 × 4 × 4 = 256** possible pure codes.

---

## 🌀 Mixed Codes

But real people are rarely "purely one type." So LoveLens introduces **mixed codes**—when your secondary tendency in a dimension is also strong, it's shown with a slash:

```
S/A - F - N - C/T
```

This code means:
- Attachment: **Primarily secure, but with anxious tendencies too**
- Expression: Pure Frame
- Structure: Pure Nested
- Conflict: **Primarily Confront, but also Think**

### Trigger Condition

When the **second-highest letter's score in a dimension is ≥ 50% of the highest letter's score**, a mixed code appears.

### Directionality

- **Attachment** and **Structure** are **bidirectional**—S/A and A/S have different meanings
- **Expression** and **Conflict** are **unidirectional**—W/D and D/W refer to the same mix

### Why It's Designed This Way

Attachment and structure are **deeper** dimensions; the order of primary and secondary matters significantly. Expression and conflict are **behavioral** dimensions; the difference in primary/secondary ordering is less significant.

# ✍️ Where the 24 Main Types Come From

There are 256 pure codes plus many mixed codes—**why did we only write 24 type descriptions**?

## 📐 How the Types Are Organized

We organize all codes into **4 families** by **attachment dimension**:

- 🔵 **Secure Family** (all codes starting with S-): 6 types
- 🔴 **Anxious Family** (all codes starting with A-): 6 types
- 🟡 **Avoidant Family** (all codes starting with V-): 6 types
- 🟣 **Disorganized Family** (all codes starting with X-): 6 types

**Total: 4 × 6 = 24 main types.**

## 🌿 Why It's Divided This Way

Because **attachment type is the most core**—it determines a person's underlying feeling in relationships. The other 3 dimensions (Expression, Structure, Conflict) are **external manifestations of this underlying feeling**.

For example:
- Same "talkative" (W): **S-W** is naturally sharing, **A-W** is anxiously confirming, **V-W** is rationally explaining
- Same "merging" (M): **S-M** is two complete people choosing deep merging, **A-M** is longing for oneness to eliminate insecurity

So although S-W-M-C and A-W-M-C differ by only one letter, **the inner feeling is completely different**—which is why the attachment dimension determines "which type you are."

## 🎨 How the 6 Types Were Chosen

Within each family, we chose 6 most representative types based on **Expression × Structure × Conflict** combinations. Not every combination is described separately—because some combinations are too similar (the difference between S-W-N-C and S-W-N-R is far less than the difference between S-W-N-C and V-F-L-Y).

We chose the 6 that **best represent the different styles within each family**.

---

## 🔍 What Does "Most Representative" Actually Mean—for the Curious Reader

If you got here and thought: "But how exactly is 'most representative' defined?"—that's a good question. Here are the three criteria we used.

### 📏 Criterion 1: Coverage

Each family has **4 × 4 × 4 = 64 possible pure code combinations** (looking only at Expression × Structure × Conflict). To choose 6 out of 64, we wanted those 6 to **cover as many different letters as possible** across the dimensions.

Take the Secure Family as an example:

| Type | Code |
|------|------|
| №1 The Clear-Eyed Co-Builder | S-D-N-C |
| №2 The Warm Anchor | S-F-M-R |
| №3 The Honest Companion | S-W-N-C |
| №4 The Silent Guardian | S-P-L-Y |
| №5 The Tender Walker | S-D-N-R |
| №6 The Deep Connector | S-W-M-C |

Breaking it down:
- Expression covers **W, D, P, F** (all 4 letters present)
- Structure covers **M, N, L** (O is missing—a trade-off)
- Conflict covers **C, R, Y** (T is missing—a trade-off)

100% coverage isn't possible because 6 < 64. But even if a letter isn't "represented" by any of the 6 types, the **matching algorithm** (see Document 12) can still find the closest type when users test into that letter.

### 📐 Criterion 2: Differentiation

Two types differing by only one letter (e.g., S-W-N-C vs S-W-N-R) are technically two types, but their descriptions would be 80% identical—readers would think "these two are too similar."

So our rule: **any two types within the same family must differ by at least 2 letters**. We'd rather sacrifice fine-grained distinctions than have 6 descriptions that feel redundant.

### 🌿 Criterion 3: Realism

Some pure code combinations **exist in theory but rarely in real life**. For example, S-P-O-Y (secure + presence-based expression + orbit structure + yielding conflict)—"secure" and "orbit + yield" are temperamentally at odds; real people are more likely to be V-P-O-Y or S-P-L-Y.

We chose the combinations **that actually show up in real lives**, not an exhaustive "all theoretically possible" set.

### 🎭 The Matrix Structure: 6 Positions, 6 Temperaments

At a deeper level, the 24 types aren't chosen at random—they form a **4 (families) × 6 (temperament positions) matrix**:

| Position | Temperament |
|----------|-------------|
| 1 | The Direct-Talker—most willing to confront |
| 2 | The Warm Merger—most drawn to deep union |
| 3 | The Clear Speaker—most articulate |
| 4 | The Quiet Independent—most internal |
| 5 | The Soft Repairer—most relationship-oriented |
| 6 | The Deep Connector—most fully invested |

All 4 families are written along these 6 positions. That's why the Secure family's №3 and the Anxious family's №3 are both "the one who talks"—but their temperaments are completely different (S-W is open sharing, A-W is anxious reassurance-seeking).

This is why the 6 types within a family feel like "the same song in different keys"—they share a family temperament while each carries a distinct relational style.

### 🙈 A Note of Honesty

In the end, "most representative" can't be fully objective. It's a blend of:

- **Data intuition**—anticipating which combinations would show up most often when designing the 48 questions
- **Writing value**—6 types differentiated enough that 6 descriptions don't repeat themselves
- **Narrative completeness**—6 positions that let each family stand as its own small universe

So a more honest framing: **these 24 types are a curated sample, not an exhaustive taxonomy.**

If your code happens to be one of the 24—great, read that one.
If your code isn't in the 24—that's fine too. The algorithm finds the **closest** match, but your complete code (including mixed codes) remains the most accurate expression. The 24 types are a familiar reference frame, not a truth.

For a deeper understanding of each letter in your code, see **Document 13 (Letter Deep Dives)**—where each letter has its own standalone description.

# 🎯 Scoring Rules

## 📝 Question Type

Each LoveLens question presents you with a **life scenario**, with **4 options** below, each corresponding to one of the 4 letters in that dimension.

## 🎲 Answering Method

Each question allows **multiple selections** (not required—you can select just 1).

**Click order = priority**. The first one you click is "most like you," the second is "also quite like me," the third is "somewhat like me."

## 📊 Score Calculation

| Click Order | Score |
|-------------|-------|
| 1st selected | **3 points** |
| 2nd selected | **2 points** |
| 3rd selected | **1 point** |
| Not selected | **0 points** |

## 🎯 Final Code Determination

For each dimension:
1. Sum the scores for each letter across all 12 questions
2. The **highest-scoring letter** becomes your primary code
3. If the **second-highest letter is ≥ 50% of the highest**, it displays as a mixed code (e.g., S/A)
4. Combine all 4 dimensions = your complete code

## 🌙 Why This Scoring System

**All 4 options can be considered.** Traditional single-choice questions can only express "most like"—but in reality, many people feel "A and B both sort of fit me." Multi-select + weighting lets you express "A fits most, B also fits"—this is why mixed codes appear.

**Sorted by click order.** This means you don't have to think "should I pick 2 or 3"—you just click in order from "most like" downward, and the system automatically handles the weighting.

# 🌙 Design Philosophy

This part is the most core "spirit" of LoveLens—and the principles we reminded ourselves of repeatedly when writing every type description.

## 🪞 Principle 1: The Test Is a Mirror, Not a Verdict

All LoveLens descriptions **don't judge you**.

We won't say "your type is healthy" or "your type has problems." We only say "**this is how you are—there's beauty in it, and there are parts that need attention**."

Because—**every type is a product of some environment**. A person becomes avoidant perhaps because they had to learn to "take care of themselves" to survive as a child. A person becomes anxious perhaps because they grew up in an environment where "love could disappear at any time."

How can you judge a pattern **someone formed to survive**?

You can only **see it, understand it, and slowly dialogue with it**.

## 🌸 Principle 2: Every Type Deserves to Be Described Beautifully

This is the most heartfelt aspect of LoveLens's tone.

Many tests describe "unhealthy" types terribly—anxious types are "clingy," avoidant types are "cold," disorganized types are "problematic."

**We don't do that.**

Every type—whatever it is—in our descriptions is **a complete, worthy-of-being-loved person**. We'll point out the challenges that type might face, but **we won't make the reader of that passage feel like they "are a problem"**.

This is because: **the people reading these descriptions are living, breathing humans**. They might be in a difficult relationship right now, might have just experienced heartbreak, might have never been described so gently before.

We hope that when they read their type, they feel—**"Ah, so that's how I am. So it's okay to be this way."**

## 🌿 Principle 3: We Don't Recommend "Ideal Partners"

You may have noticed that LoveLens doesn't tell you "who you should look for."

We only tell you: **"Which people naturally click with you, and which people might require some adjustment."**

Why? Because **true love isn't "finding the right person"—it's "two people choosing to learn how to love together"**.

If we told you "you're S-type, you should find S-type," that would be saying "people of other types aren't worthy of your love"—this isn't the logic of love, this is consumer logic.

**In real relationships, two people who aren't particularly "compatible" can go very far—as long as they're willing to adjust for each other. Two "perfectly matched" people can also fail to make it—if neither is willing to change.**

So LoveLens only describes **differences**, it doesn't make **recommendations**.

## 💫 Principle 4: You Are Different Selves with Different People

This is the last thing LoveLens wants to tell you.

Your test result is **a baseline**—what you're like in a "neutral state." But in real relationships, your partner will **activate different parts of you**.

- Some people make you more stable, more clear
- Some people make you suddenly anxious, unusually so
- Some people make you open up sides you never show

This isn't "you changed"—it's that **every person brings out different things in you**.

So this test result isn't the final answer—it's **a starting point**.

In your real relationships, feel it: **What kind of me does this person make me? Do I like who I am when I'm with this person?**

That's the most important question.

---

## 🌷 A Final Word

LoveLens isn't perfect. No test can fully capture a person's complete complexity.

If, when reading a passage, you feel **"Ah, this is me"**—that resonance is the meaning of the test.

If, when reading a passage, you feel **"this isn't quite me"**—that inconsistency is also the meaning of the test. Because it makes you more clearly know: **"So what am I actually like?"**

This question has no final answer. But asking this question itself is understanding yourself more deeply.

And people who understand themselves more deeply usually also know better how to love. 🌙
