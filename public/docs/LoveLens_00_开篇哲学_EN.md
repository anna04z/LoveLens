# 🌷 A few things we want to say, before you begin

---

## 🪞 This is just a test

**Just a test** — not a verdict, not a label, not a tool that defines who you are.

48 questions can't hold a whole person. You are more complex, more layered, more lovable than any code, any type, any description.

So when you read the results — **feel free to resonate, but don't feel obligated to believe it all**. If a passage makes you think "ah, that's exactly me" — that's it. If a passage feels wrong — let it sit for a while. Maybe it's not your core, or maybe it's a side of you you haven't seen yet.

**Both are OK.**

---

## 🍃 The answers you're picking now are the "calm you"

We want to say this at the very start.

Right now, you're probably sitting down, holding a cup of something, maybe curled up on a couch — **your mind is quiet**. So you read each question, think it through, and pick the answer that feels "most like you."

But the you in a relationship isn't always this you.

- When you really fall for someone, you might become someone you barely recognize
- When someone runs hot and cold with you, you might do things you'd "never normally do"
- Around a certain person, you might suddenly feel insecure, petty, hypersensitive — even though that's not usually you
- After being hurt, you might curl inward, into a version of yourself that feels like a stranger

**So this test measures the "calm you, right now" — not the "you in love."**

Those two people are sometimes similar, sometimes very different. Both are you.

When you read the results, leave a little room for the "you who loses control" — that part of you deserves to be understood too, not corrected.

---

## 💫 You become a different you, with different people

This is the most important thing we want you to remember.

Your "type" isn't fixed forever.

- With someone who makes you feel safe, you become steadier, more expressive, more willing to give
- With someone who makes you anxious, you might suddenly become anxious, off-balance, withdrawn
- With someone who truly gets you, you open up sides of yourself you didn't know existed
- With someone who drains you, you become a stiffened, unfamiliar version of yourself

**None of this means "you changed" — different people bring out different things in you.**

So if you're "secure" with A and "anxious" with B — both are you. Neither is "the real you." **Every relationship is shaping the you of that moment.**

---

## 🌱 Types shift with life stages

Who you are at 20 won't be who you are at 30.

- After a painful relationship, you might move from "secure" toward "avoidant" for a while
- After being truly loved, you might move slowly from "anxious" toward "secure"
- After certain growth, even your answer to "what kind of relationship do I want?" may change

**This test measures who you are *now* — not who you'll always be.**

So if you take it again in a few years, the result might be different. That's not the test failing — that's you growing, changing, walking further.

---

## 🌸 There are no "good" or "bad" types

We want to say this three times.

**There are no good types.**
**There are no bad types.**
**People just love in different ways.**

- Secure isn't "the right answer"
- Anxious isn't "something to fix"
- Avoidant isn't "not knowing how to love"
- Disorganized isn't "broken"

Every type has its beauty, its challenges, its reasons to be held gently.

**This test isn't looking for "the healthy template" — it's helping you see how you love.**

Then you can carry that understanding into your relationships.

---

## 🌿 The most important thing: love is about meeting each other halfway

This might be the single **most important** thing LoveLens wants to say:

**There's no such thing as two people who are "naturally meant for each other" — only two people who are willing to meet each other halfway.**

Two people who seem to "match perfectly" won't last if neither adjusts.
Two people who seem "mismatched" can go far, if they're willing to learn for each other.

**What matters most in love isn't finding the right person — it's two people slowly becoming the right person for each other, together.**

That's why this test won't tell you "who to date" — it just tells you: **"This is who you are, and these are the differences you might encounter."** The rest —

**is the space between you and them, where you meet each other halfway.**

## 💌 One last thing — something a little more personal

If you've read this far — thank you for staying with me through all of that.

LoveLens started, honestly, as a question I had for myself.

I've always carried a deep fear of marriage and intimacy. I often think I wouldn't be a good partner — there's too much I'm not yet, too many wounds I've carried, too much about myself I'm still learning to face. I thought: if there were a test that could help me see "what I'm like, in love," maybe I could understand myself a little more, and maybe be a little gentler with whoever walks into my life one day.

But I couldn't find a test like that. Most were either too clinical, or too "one right answer," or too quick to sort people into "the good ones" and "the ones who need fixing."

So I thought — I'll just make one myself.

That's where LoveLens came from. Not a professional tool built by a psychologist — a mirror made by someone who's afraid of love, and who also wants to love well, for herself and for anyone else walking a similar road.

My own result is **S/A - W/P - M/N - T/C** — closest to "the tender connector." But I know that's just me sitting calmly at a screen. In a real relationship, I might be anxious, clumsy, unable to hold myself together.

**I'm still learning to understand myself. Still learning to be less afraid.**

If you're somewhere on a similar road — I hope this mirror meets you gently.

— Anna
