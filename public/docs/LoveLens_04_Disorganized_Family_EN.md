# 🟣 Disorganized Family
## LoveLens Types №19 - №24 Complete Descriptions

**Shared Traits:** There's a pull inside you—longing to get close, and afraid of getting close. This isn't weakness, it's **the result of experiencing things that made you both love and fear**. Your love may swing widely, may go back and forth, may be something you can't even articulate yourself—but that's also a real kind of love.

---

# №19 🌊 The Emotional Tide

**Code:** X-W-M-C

**Soul Signature:**
> "I want to fully possess you, and fear being possessed by you."

---

## 🌿 About You

You're the kind of person who **loves like rising tide, recedes like ebbing tide**. Your emotions have their own rhythm—sometimes you fully invest, with all your heart, wanting to put your partner in your pocket; sometimes you suddenly need distance, need space, want to be alone. These two states **switch back and forth** in you, sometimes even on the same day.

Your love is very intense, very sincere—but also exhausts you. You want unity, want two people to become one—but when it gets truly close, you instinctively want to pull back. Not because you don't love, but because **"too close" itself makes you uneasy**.

You're not deliberately hot and cold. You truly **feel this way one moment, that way the next**. Sometimes you yourself don't understand what you're thinking—in the morning "this is what I want," in the afternoon "I need to breathe." There are often **two voices fighting** inside you: one says "closer," one says "further back."

In expression, you're very direct—you say you love, say you want, say your feelings. But these feelings change quickly. When you say "I'll love you forever" it's real, three days later when you say "I need to be alone" it's also real. **Both are real**.

Your deepest challenge is—**your emotional swings may exhaust your partner**. Not because you're bad, but because they need to learn **"the you right now isn't all of you."** But you have to learn this yourself first.

---

## 🪞 Similar but Different Types

- **№7 The Passionate Pursuer**: Both love fiercely and express directly. But №7 is a straight line—once loving, keeps loving; your love has tides, rises and falls.

**This swinging—sometimes even you yourself can't articulate it, can you?**

---

## 💫 How You Show Up in Relationships

- You love deeply, and retreat quickly
- When you say love, it's real; when you say "I need space," that's also real
- Your emotional swings sometimes leave your partner at a loss
- Sometimes you don't understand yourself
- You long for intimacy, and fear it

---

## ✨ Your Strengths

💎 **Authentic emotion**
You don't perform, don't fake, don't suppress; you feel genuinely, express genuinely.

💎 **Deep investment**
When you love, you truly hand yourself over completely.

💎 **Courage to express**
You dare say "I love you," and dare say "I need space right now"—both take courage.

💎 **Rich inner world**
Your emotional world is deeper than most people's; this is your romance and your weight.

---

## 🌱 Things You Might Notice

🌷 **Your feelings change faster than you realize**
The morning "I want forever" and the afternoon "I need space" may both be real. But your partner only sees "you don't keep your word."

🌷 **You instinctively want to retreat from "too close"**
When the relationship is going well, feelings are good, you suddenly want to pull back. No one's fault—it's your reflex, one even you find strange.

🌷 **You may pull your partner into your swings**
Your emotional waves affect them. They may need time to learn not to go up and down with your tides.

🌷 **You're learning to understand yourself too**
You're not deliberately confusing them—you're confused yourself. This takes time.

---

## 🌸 Partners Who Naturally Click with You

🕊️ **Partners who can understand your swings**
→ They know your "retreat" isn't not loving, it's your rhythm; they don't panic during your temporary distance.

🕊️ **Emotionally stable, self-anchored partners**
→ Your tides need a stable shore; they don't get carried away by your swings, but can catch you.

🕊️ **Partners willing to have deep conversation with you**
→ You need to "truly talk it through"; meeting someone willing to discuss souls is what lets you settle.

🕊️ **Partners with their own lives, not waiting on you every moment**
→ When you need space, they don't collapse; they have their own center, so you two aren't death-locked.

🕊️ **Partners who directly express needs**
→ You're not good at guessing; when they directly say "I hope for this," you can respond.

🕊️ **Partners who understand your contradictions but don't try to "cure" you**
→ They accept your complexity, not wanting to remake you; this itself is a deep form of love.

---

## 🍃 Partners Who May Require Adjustment

🌿 **Partners especially stable, always at the same temperature**
→ Their stability is an anchor to you, but they may also be confused by your swings. Not cold—they just haven't experienced your way of feeling.

🌿 **Partners who interpret your "retreat" as "not loving"**
→ You retreat to recharge, not to push them away. Both sides need to slowly learn this.

🌿 **Partners wanting you to be consistent**
→ You can't do "consistent"—your truth is the swinging. They need to learn to appreciate your complexity, but you can also try to explain "I'm not changing my heart."

🌿 **Partners with very dense emotional needs**
→ When they need soothing and you happen to be in a "retreat" cycle, you feel helpless. Tell them: "I'm not in my best state right now, but I'm not gone."

🌿 **Overly anxious partners**
→ Their anxiety meeting your swings doubles up. Two sensitive people need extra gentleness.

🌿 **Partners wanting a waveless relationship**
→ Your relationship will always have waves—this is what you bring. This isn't wrong, but they need to be able to handle it.

# №20 🌌 The Searching Idealist

**Code:** X-P-O-R

**Soul Signature:**
> "Perfect love is out there somewhere, but I'm not sure I can hold onto it."

---

## 🌿 About You

You're the kind of person with **very high imagination for love**—not materially high, but **at the soul level**. You believe "the one" truly exists—someone who can read you, match you, with whom everything feels right. You're unwilling to settle, unwilling to enter a "good enough" relationship—**you want "the right one."**

But you also find—every time you seem to meet "that person," after a while you start doubting again. Is something wrong here? Is something lacking there? Is there actually "someone more right" elsewhere? You swing between investing and withdrawing—**not because you don't love, but because you fear missing out**.

Your love is a kind of **present-but-not-fully-present** love. You accompany, you love—but part of your heart is always **searching**: is this right? Could it be better? This searching may make your partner feel "part of you isn't here," but you yourself may not realize.

When problems arise in the relationship, you tend to **soothe the atmosphere first** rather than face directly. Not that you don't want to solve, but you fear **"after talking you'll discover this isn't the right person after all."** So you'd rather let ambiguity continue—in ambiguity, hope still exists.

Your deepest contradiction is—**you long for "the right one," but everyone eventually disappoints you**. Not because they're not good, but because **no one can constantly match "perfection."** And there's a ruler in your heart, and that ruler is always measuring.

---

## 🪞 Similar but Different Types

- **№19 The Emotional Tide**: Both swing and retreat. But №19's swinging is **changes in feeling**; yours is more **changes in doubt about "is this right."**

**Don't you always have a ruler in your heart, measuring this person in front of you?**

---

## 💫 How You Show Up in Relationships

- You invest, but part of you always searches
- You're very sensitive to relationship details—every "not right" gets recorded
- In conflict, you tend to soothe the atmosphere, unwilling to face "possible truths"
- You go back and forth between "this is the right person" and "maybe it's not"
- You fear settling, but also fear missing out

---

## ✨ Your Strengths

💎 **Sincerity about love**
You don't want to make do; this unwillingness itself is a respect for love.

💎 **Keen perception**
You see subtle differences many people can't see.

💎 **Deep imagination**
You can paint the most beautiful version of what a relationship could be.

💎 **Courage not to settle**
You won't stay in the wrong relationship because of loneliness; this takes great courage.

---

## 🌱 Things You Might Notice

🌷 **"The perfect one" may always be at the next intersection**
Every time you get close, you find new "not rights"; this isn't your partner's problem, it's your way of searching.

🌷 **Your searching makes your partner feel you're "not fully there"**
You may be sitting across from them, but thinking "is this really right?"; they can feel it.

🌷 **You fear facing "it's just not right"**
If you really talk, really see—the fantasy of "the right one" will shatter. So you let ambiguity continue.

🌷 **The ruler in your heart may not be measuring your partner, but yourself**
The pursuit of perfection sometimes is because you feel you don't deserve less—or feel you don't deserve something too good.

---

## 🌸 Partners Who Naturally Click with You

🕊️ **Partners also with soul depth**
→ You don't need someone to "just live with"; you need someone who can discuss souls with you.

🕊️ **Emotionally stable partners who won't swing with you**
→ Your swinging needs an anchor; when they firmly stay there, you can slowly stop.

🕊️ **Partners willing to discuss "what we are" with you**
→ You have many question marks in relationships; when they don't avoid these questions, you can truly be at ease.

🕊️ **Partners who don't try to "fulfill all your expectations"**
→ They're themselves, not some image you fantasize; this authenticity matters more than perfection.

🕊️ **Patient partners who don't rush to define the relationship**
→ You need time to switch from "searching" to "staying"; they can wait for you.

🕊️ **Partners who can gently say "I know you're searching, but I'm here"**
→ They aren't scared off by your searching; their firmness slowly stops you.

---

## 🍃 Partners Who May Require Adjustment

🌿 **Partners with more jumpy emotions**
→ You're already swinging inside; if they're also unstable, the relationship has no anchor.

🌿 **Partners wanting to "confirm the relationship" immediately**
→ You need time; they may want quick progression. Different paces—both need patience.

🌿 **Partners too easily satisfied with "good enough"**
→ Their "good enough" may feel like settling to you. You need someone who can understand "I just don't settle."

🌿 **Partners with very dense emotional needs**
→ You're already swinging between "here and not here"; their dense needs make you want to retreat more.

🌿 **Partners wanting you fully invested, nothing held back**
→ Hard for you to "fully be there"; they need to accept your "part staying in your heart."

🌿 **Partners unwilling to talk deeply with you**
→ You need deep conversation to confirm the relationship; their shallow interaction makes you feel "not right."

# №21 🎭 The Conscious Torn

**Code:** X-W-N-T

**Soul Signature:**
> "I know I both want and don't want. I'm figuring it out."

---

## 🌿 About You

You're the kind with **the most self-awareness** in the disorganized family—you **know you're contradictory**. You can see the pull between wanting to get close and wanting to run, can describe your own conflicts, can maintain an observer outside emotions. This awareness makes you different from other disorganized types—**you're on your way to understanding yourself**.

You're not a slave to emotions. When you feel like retreating, you ask yourself "why do I want to retreat?"; when you feel especially wanting closeness, you ask yourself "is this really love or my anxiety?" You treat yourself as an object you can research, understand, slowly draw close to—**this seriousness about yourself is what many people never learn in a lifetime**.

You're also willing to process yourself. You don't dump all chaos onto your partner. You think it through first, sort it out, understand it, then express it more clearly. You believe in "communication"—but you also believe, **you have to understand yourself first, for others to understand you**.

But your awareness doesn't mean **those feelings disappear**. You still have moments of wanting to retreat, still doubt yourself, still fear in love. **Awareness and feeling can coexist**—you live between "I know I'm scared right now" and "but I'm still scared." This isn't contradiction, this is the real shape of growth.

---

- **№19 The Emotional Tide**: Both swing. But №19 is **pulled along by emotions**; you're **watching yourself being pulled, slowly coming back**.

**Don't you often feel, while observing yourself feel?**

---

# №22 🦋 The Moth to Flame

**Code:** X-D-M-Y

**Soul Signature:**
> "I know I might get hurt, but I'll still throw myself in."

---

## 🌿 About You

You're the kind who, **once in love, throws yourself in completely**—even when you clearly know you might get hurt. Your love is a **burning**: hot, reckless, easy to burn yourself up.

You're not without alertness. On the contrary, you often have a premonition of **"this might not end well"**—certain signals from the other person make you wary, certain patterns make you subtly uneasy. But you still go. Not because you're stupid, but because **"not going" is harder for you**. Staying safely outside hurts more than getting hurt in danger.

You love through action. You do a lot, give a lot, transform yourself into what they need. You're willing to let go of a lot—your own boundaries, your own needs, your own judgment—just to be with this person. In conflict, you tend to **yield**—not because they're right, but because you fear "if I don't yield, you'll leave."

Your deepest contradiction is—**you know you're getting smaller in this kind of relationship, but you can't stop**. There's a very big longing in your heart—longing to be loved, to be chosen, **to have "this time be different."** This longing pushes you to throw yourself in, again and again.

You're not weak. You're **someone who loves too hard**—so hard you hurt yourself and didn't stop.

**Don't you often know you shouldn't continue, but still can't stop?**

# №23 🌑 The Silent Paradox

**Code:** X-P-L-Y

**Soul Signature:**
> "I want to get close to you, but I can't speak it, and I can't walk over."

---

## 🌿 About You

You're the **quietest, most suppressed** kind in the disorganized family. You have very deep longings inside—to be loved, to get close, to be truly seen. But these longings, you **find hard to speak, and hard to act on**. You stand there, watching the person you want to approach, but can't take a single step.

Your contradiction isn't externalized like №19 (The Emotional Tide)—you don't tide in or out, you **stop in one place, while your insides are tearing**. Your partner can't see your churning; what they see is a calm, quiet, even slightly distant you. But inside you're always saying—"**come closer, see me, let me come close to you**"—you just can't say these words.

You also don't take initiative. Not because you don't want to, but because **"initiative" itself is too hard for you**—fearing rejection, fearing saying the wrong thing, fearing they actually don't want you. So you choose the safest method: **waiting**. Waiting for them to come ask, to come close, to come see the longing you haven't spoken.

When they finally walk over, you may instinctively step back. Not because you don't want it, but because **being truly seen scares you**. You're used to longing, but not used to being satisfied. This is a very lonely state—what you want is always there, but you keep preventing yourself from having it.

In conflict, you tend to **yield, disappear, digest alone**. You don't fight, don't argue, don't express dissatisfaction—you withdraw into your own world and digest those feelings alone. Others see you as fine, but your heart is actually full.

---

## 🪞 Similar but Different Types

- **№18 The Quiet Shore**: Both don't initiate, are quiet, yield. But №18 is avoidant—their quiet is "being there is enough"; you're disorganized—beneath your quiet **you're always longing for closeness, and always blocked by yourself**.
- **№12 The Tender Bird**: Both long inside to be seen, don't initiate. But №12's longing is **relatively clear**—they know they want to be loved; your longing and fear **exist simultaneously**—wanting to get close and run, so you're not sure yourself what you want.

**Don't you often think a lot inside, but can't say a word?**

---

## 💫 How You Show Up in Relationships

- You rarely take initiative, waiting for them
- You have many feelings inside, few come out
- When they truly come close, you may instinctively step back
- In conflict, you choose to disappear—not walking away, but shrinking yourself
- You look calm, but inside you're actually full

---

## ✨ Your Strengths

💎 **Deep inner world**
You feel more, finer, deeper than others; this is the gift of sensitivity.

💎 **Not draining your partner**
You don't pressure, demand, cling; they're relaxed around you.

💎 **Authentic longing**
Your heart's yearning for love is real—no pretending, no performing.

💎 **Gentle presence**
Though you don't speak, they can feel you; this low-key presence has its weight.

---
