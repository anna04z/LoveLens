# 💕 LoveLens · Mixed Code Explanation

> If your test result includes a slash (like **S/A**, **W/D**, **N/M**), it means you're not "purely one type" in that dimension—you have two tendencies present at the same time.
>
> **This isn't the test being inaccurate**, and it's not "you swinging between two"—this is **two sides you've always had**.
>
> This document helps you understand: how these two tendencies work together in you.

---

## 📐 How to Read Mixed Codes

**Trigger condition:** When the second-highest letter in a dimension scores ≥ 50% of the highest letter, a mixed code appears.

**Directionality:**
- **Attachment dimension** (S/A/V/X) and **Structure dimension** (M/N/L/O) are **bidirectional**—S/A and A/S mean different things
- **Expression dimension** (W/D/P/F) and **Conflict dimension** (C/T/R/Y) are **unidirectional**—only indicating primary/secondary, not distinguishing W/D from D/W

**How to read your own code:**
- First letter = **primary tendency** (highest score)
- After the slash = **secondary tendency** (but prominent enough that it can't be ignored)

# 🔵 Attachment Dimension Mixes (Bidirectional)

---

## 🌹 S/A — Secure primary, Anxious secondary

**Signature:** I'm basically stable, but once I care about someone, my heart still races, I still worry.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
You're stable most of the time. You know how to express, how to communicate, how to be a "good partner" in a relationship. But **when you truly fall for someone**—that small voice deep inside still speaks up: "why haven't they replied today?" "did I say something wrong?" Your anxiety isn't dominant; it only comes out when you especially care. So from the outside you always look steady, but you yourself know—**it's not that you have no anxiety, you've learned to live with it**.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You're a bit strict with yourself about "not being supposed to be anxious." The feeling clearly comes, but you tell yourself "I should be more steady." But anxiety's appearance doesn't mean you've regressed—**it just means you care**. Allow it to appear, and it won't overwhelm you.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Steadiness isn't the absence of swings; it's not losing your way within the swings. You've always been on your own side.

## 🌸 A/S — Anxious primary, Secure secondary

**Signature:** I get anxious, I worry, but I'm also learning to steady myself.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your base is anxious—you think a lot, care a lot, worry a lot in relationships. But you're not the kind who is **fully pulled along by anxiety**. You have a "clear-eyed self" observing on the side—when unease comes, you can pause and think "is this real, or am I worrying?" You're on your way from anxious to secure. **Sometimes you can steady yourself, sometimes you still churn**—both are real.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may be disappointed in yourself because "I'm not fully steady yet." But growth isn't achieved overnight—having more steady moments than a year ago is progress. Don't use "ideal state" to judge "the you that's growing."

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> You don't have to be "fully good" to deserve love. The you who's learning is already enough.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice

> Your distance is a kind of wisdom, but the step beyond distance is also worth taking occasionally.

# 🟢 Expression Dimension Mixes (Unidirectional)

Expression dimension is unidirectional—W/D and D/W refer to the same combination, indicating you mainly use two ways to express love, without distinguishing which is more dominant.

---

## 💬 W/D — Words + Doing

**Signature:** I can say "I love you," and I can also quietly get things done.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your way of expressing love is very comprehensive—you **both say and do**. Your partner won't feel you're all talk and no action (because you really do things), nor that you only act without expressing (because you also say it). This is a balanced combination. Your partner most likely feels **fully loved**—verbally confirmed, and proven through action.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Because you do both well, you may have hidden expectations of your partner—"I've said it and done it, why can't you respond?" But not everyone can do both at once. Allow your partner to love you in their own way.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Your love is like a dinner with both main course and dessert. While abundant, also remember you deserve to be served too.

---

## 🌿 W/P — Words + Presence

**Signature:** I'll say it, and I'll be there. Present when speaking, speaking when present.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your expression is very flowing—**you can both articulate love clearly with words, and have the ability to "be there"**. You're not someone who proves love with gifts or actions, nor the type who only speaks without doing—you live love as a **combination of presence + language**. When they're with you, they feel heard, seen, accompanied.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may feel "I've said it and been there" is enough—but some partners need to see concrete action evidence. Not that you're not doing enough, but different people receive love on different channels. Occasionally doing something concrete will make your love more three-dimensional.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Spoken words settle, companion time ferments. The love you give is the kind that slowly ripens.

---

## 🛠️ W/F — Words + Frame

**Signature:** I'll express love, and seriously build this "us."

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
You both speak love and **think clearly about the structure of the relationship**. You're not someone who only discusses feelings—you also discuss future, boundaries, expectations, responsibilities with your partner. Your love has warmth and blueprint. This makes you very suited for long-term relationships—because you're not just "feeling is enough," you're willing to **seriously build the relationship**.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may mix feelings and discussion—your partner wants comfort, but you start discussing "what should we do?" Occasionally give empathy first, then talk structure. Not every conversation needs a solution.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> You're both the one who speaks and the one who builds. You make love both felt and seen.

---

## 🌸 D/P — Doing + Presence

**Signature:** I don't say much, but I do, I'm there.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
You're someone who **expresses love through action and presence**—doesn't talk much, but your partner can feel your love. You silently do things, silently accompany, silently appear at their needed moments. Your love is **quiet but very concrete**. Your partner may initially feel you're not enthusiastic enough, but after a long time realize—you're the kind who "doesn't say but is always there," a deep reliability.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Some partners need verbal confirmation. They see what you do, but inside they ask "do you actually love me?" Occasionally saying it—not changing yourself, giving them a supplementary signal.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Your love is like a tree's presence. Doesn't speak, but is always there.

---

## 🏛️ D/F — Doing + Frame

**Signature:** I love you through action, and protect us through structure.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
You **both do and build structure**—you not only take action for your partner, you also **seriously manage the relationship itself**. You'll discuss rules, boundaries, long-term plans. You're the kind who "puts love into practice"—not just emotion, but actual maintenance. Your partner feels **real security** being with you.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may not be good at romantic expression—don't express feelings, don't speak sweet words. But some partners need those "being moved" moments. Occasionally let love overflow from structure—not changing yourself, adding a dimension.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> You love in concrete ways. Those "small things" are actually evidence of your love.

---

## 🌙 P/F — Presence + Frame

**Signature:** I'm there, and I'll seriously think through this "us" thing.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your expression style is **company plus clarity**—you won't use many words to prove love, but you'll **be there**. At the same time, you have a clear attitude toward the relationship—what's okay, what isn't, where we're going. This makes you both steady and clear-eyed in the relationship. Being with you, your partner feels **both accompanied and seriously treated**.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may lean too much toward "structured company"—a little less romance, a little less surprise, a little less "drawing close for no reason." Occasionally use no rules, just suddenly hug them—that's also love.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Your presence is a commitment. Occasionally allow that commitment to be emotional, not designed.

# 🟡 Structure Dimension Mixes (Bidirectional)

Structure dimension is bidirectional—M/N and N/M mean different things. The former means "merging primary, but also with separate space"; the latter means "separate space primary, but also with moments of merging."

---

## 🌸 M/N — Merging primary, Nested secondary

**Signature:** I hope we're very very close, but also give each other a little breathing room.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your core longing is **two people becoming one**—sharing daily life, sharing emotions, sharing life. But you have more spatial awareness than the pure merging type—you know complete merging leaves both breathless, so you leave a little "separate part" for each other. Your relationship density is very high, but not suffocating.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may be very present during intimacy, but when your partner wants a little "time for themselves," you churn inside: "did I do something wrong?" Actually they just need to recharge. When they come back, they won't love you less.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> You love tightly. But tight love also needs a crack to breathe.

---

## 🌿 N/M — Nested primary, Merging secondary

**Signature:** I want two people to be two whole people, but also deeply connected.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your default is nested—**two independent people have overlap**, not becoming one. You value each having their own life, friends, interests. But you're not a cold kind of nested—you **also have moments of merging**, when two people are very close, indistinguishable. Your relationship shape is **mostly independent, with windows of deep connection**.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may switch between "independent" and "merging" faster than your partner can keep up. Today you're very intimate, tomorrow you need space—they may be confused. Let them know your rhythm, and they can keep up better.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Independence and closeness aren't opposed. You can want both.

---

## 🌹 M/L — Merging primary, Parallel secondary

**Signature:** I want to merge, but certain parts still walk their own way.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
This is a **rare combination**—you long for merging, yet retain some parallel parts. Maybe emotionally you long for oneness, but in certain areas (work, interests, friends) you're very independent. You have **a private island within intimacy**, but this isn't withdrawal, it's your wholeness.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Your partner may be confused—sometimes you cling tightly, sometimes you don't let them into certain areas. Clearly telling them "I want to do this one thing alone" isn't rejection, it's a boundary.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Having private territory within love doesn't mean less love. It means you're still you.

---

## 🏛️ L/M — Parallel primary, Merging secondary

**Signature:** We each walk our own paths, but our paths intersect.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your primary form is parallel—**two people each have their own trajectories**, not needing to connect every moment. But you **allow occasional deep merging**—in certain moments, you completely unify with your partner. This mode suits **two very independent people who also truly love each other**.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may lean too much parallel—"each walking" becomes "barely communicating." Occasionally ask your partner: "are we close enough lately?" is a healthy calibration.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Parallel paths can walk side by side for a long time. But occasionally let them intersect—those moments prove your existence.

---

## 🌊 M/O — Merging primary, Orbit secondary

**Signature:** I want to be very close to you, but I also need moments where I can see you but not touch you.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
This is an **interesting combination**—your core longs for merging, but **you also need some distance**. Maybe you easily over-invest in intimacy, so you need "orbit-style" distance to protect yourself. You love deeply but are wary of being fully consumed.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may suddenly need "a little distance" at the most intense point of the relationship—your partner will be confused. Not that you've stopped loving, you're protecting yourself. Tell them this is your rhythm, not your withdrawal.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> What you need isn't to escape love; it's for love not to drown you.

---

## 🌌 O/M — Orbit primary, Merging secondary

**Signature:** I'm used to keeping distance, but when I love, I suddenly want to fully draw close.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your default is orbit—**in their life, but not fully entering**. But you're not cold—**you have moments of suddenly wanting to merge**. Those moments you're especially intimate, invested, as if you've become a different person. Then after a while, you return to your orbit. This pattern both fascinates and confuses your partner.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Your partner may expect the "merging you" to sustain—but that's not your normal state. Clearly tell them what your orbit mode is, and they won't feel abandoned when you return to orbit.

### 💌 A Gentle Word

## 🌿 N/L — Nested primary, Parallel secondary

**Signature:** We have overlap, and we have our own space.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
You like **two people having common parts and independent parts**. You don't want to fully merge (that suffocates) or be fully parallel (too far). You found **a comfortable balance between "we" and "me"**. This is a mature relationship form—both intimate and independent.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may be especially sensitive to **losing balance**—whether your partner is too close or too far, you immediately adjust. But occasionally letting the relationship flow on its own, not always calibrating, is also healthy.

### 💌 A Gentle Word

## 🏛️ L/N — Parallel primary, Nested secondary

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your main tone is parallel—**two independent people**, but you **also have nested parts**. You and your partner aren't one, but you have common friends, common interests, common daily life. You enjoy "we have our world, but that world is created by two independent people together."

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Some partners will feel "too independent"—they want more "we." You can ask them: "do you feel our shared parts are enough?" Let them tell you their needs.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Your partner may feel "my proportion in your life is too small." This isn't your fault, it's your rhythm. But consider: does your partner's need match your rhythm? If not, you need communication or reconsidering the relationship.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Your love is star distance—far, but always shining.

---

## 🌌 O/L — Orbit primary, Parallel secondary

**Signature:** I'm used to watching you from afar, but in certain moments we walk side by side.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your default is orbit—**in the relationship, but keeping distance**. You **occasionally walk to parallel**—experiencing things with your partner, making certain decisions together. But you don't merge; you always keep your own position. This is a **very independent kind of love**.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Some partners will feel you're mysterious and hard to truly "grasp." They need your signals—when you're in orbit, when you're willing to draw close. Clearly telling them your rhythm helps the relationship.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Orbit isn't coldness; it's how you love your partner—letting them exist freely, while watching them.

# 🔴 Conflict Dimension Mixes (Unidirectional)

Conflict dimension is unidirectional—C/T and T/C refer to the same combination, indicating you mainly use two ways to handle conflict, without distinguishing which is more dominant.

---

## ⚔️ C/T — Confront + Think

**Signature:** I'll talk directly, but I need to think clearly first before talking.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your way of handling conflict is **think first, then talk**—you won't flee (because you'll face it), but you won't be impulsive either (because you need to think first). You're very suited to relationships that "can talk things through"—you can put problems on the table, but won't let emotions lead.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Sometimes you "think" too long—your partner is waiting for you to speak, but you're still rehearsing in your head. Give yourself a time limit—24 hours of thinking is enough, then say it.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Your conversation is slow-simmered soup—but left unbrought to the table too long, it gets cold too.

---

## 🌿 C/R — Confront + Repair

**Signature:** I'll talk it out clearly, but I care more about getting us back.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
You **handle problems head-on**, but your goal isn't "who's right, who's wrong"—it's **relationship repair**. When you talk, you're gentle but sincere; after arguments, you actively propose reconciliation. This is a very healthy conflict mode—neither avoiding nor hurting.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
You may want to repair before your partner is ready—"we're okay now, right?" Let your partner digest—repair can't be rushed.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> You can both talk and hug. You turn conflict into an entrance for closeness.

---

## 🌾 C/Y — Confront + Yield

**Signature:** I'll talk, but at a certain point I'll step back.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
You don't avoid conflict—you'll talk. But you're **not the "must win" type**—when talking to a certain point, if stepping back lets the relationship continue, you step back. Your principle is: **the relationship matters more than who's right**. This is an ability only mature people have.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Yielding can be healthy or suppressive. Check yourself: are you yielding because you truly don't care, or because you fear conflict? If the latter, that yielding will pile up in your heart.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Yielding isn't losing—you chose love over your own position.

---

## 🧠 T/R — Think + Repair

**Signature:** I need to think clearly first, then figure out how to get us back.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
Your default mode after conflict is—**withdraw and think first, then actively repair**. You won't immediately fight, nor flee—you'll **digest in your own world, then return to your partner**. Your repair is thought through, so it's usually very fitting.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Your partner may be anxious while you're "thinking"—they don't know what you're doing. Tell them "I need two days to think, then we'll talk"—let them wait for you with peace of mind.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Your thinking is preparation work for love. But remember to tell your partner you're preparing.

---

## 🌌 T/Y — Think + Yield

**Signature:** I'll think a long time in my heart, then... forget it.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
This is a **combination needing care**—your way of handling conflict is "think first, then yield after thinking." Sometimes it's wisdom (you saw clearly this thing isn't worth fighting), but **more often it's avoidance** (you convince yourself "forget it" just to not talk). You may have accumulated many "unspoken thoughts" in the relationship.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Among those "forget it" moments, were there some that shouldn't have been "forgotten"? Those things leave traces in your heart. If accumulated too much, they'll erupt in unexpected ways someday—or slowly wither the relationship.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Some things are worth "letting go," some aren't. Learn to distinguish—this is your love for yourself.

---

## 🌸 R/Y — Repair + Yield

**Signature:** I'll find a way to make us better, even if I have to yield a bit.

### 🌿 What This Mix Looks Like
After conflict you **actively repair**—cooking, messaging, hugging. And you're **willing to yield**—for the relationship to improve, you don't count too much. You're the "gentle peacemaker" in the relationship—not without principle, you truly value the relationship highly.

### 🪞 What You Might Notice
Long-term this lets your partner take "repair and yield" as default—they may not learn to repair themselves. Gently tell them: "this time you come find me"—not revenge, balance.

### 💌 A Gentle Word
> Your repair is precious. But repair needs two people to catch, not just you throwing.

## ✨ Finally

If you have mixed codes in multiple dimensions—**you're not "more complex," you're more **dimensional**. Pure types rarely exist. In most people, several tendencies operate simultaneously.

What this document helps you see is: **those "seemingly contradictory" parts of you actually have their own logic**. You can want to get close and run away; can both speak and act; can be independent and long for merging.

**These aren't contradictions—this is wholeness**.
